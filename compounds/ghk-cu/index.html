Research overview

A naturally occurring tripeptide found in mammalian blood that carries a copper atom. In skin cell and animal research models, it has been studied for its ability to upregulate the genes responsible for producing collagen and elastin — the structural proteins that give tissue its strength and elasticity. Studies have also examined its antioxidant activity and its role in regulating enzymes involved in tissue remodelling. One of the most researched copper-peptide compounds in dermatological science. For in vitro research use only. HPLC purity 99.0%.

Summary paraphrased from the peer-reviewed preclinical literature. Full citations will be available in the research library. For in vitro research use only.
