Metabolic research peptides

Metabolic research peptides are studied for their roles in regulating energy balance, insulin response, glucose metabolism, and mitochondrial signalling across preclinical and in vitro models.

This category includes Retatrutide, a next-generation triple-receptor agonist simultaneously engaging GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon pathways; Mots-C, a mitochondrially-encoded peptide investigated for AMPK activation; and NAD+, the foundational coenzyme at the centre of sirtuin enzyme activity and cellular energy metabolism. Research across this category examines receptor-mediated signalling, metabolic gene expression, and the interactions between nutrient-sensing pathways.

For in vitro research use only. Compounds in this category are not for human or veterinary consumption, diagnosis, treatment, cure, or prevention of any condition.

Compounds in metabolic research

Metabolic

Mots-C

Mitochondrial Open Reading Frame Peptide
98.5% HPLC

A 16-amino-acid peptide with an unusual origin — its genetic instructions come from the DNA inside mitochondria (the cell's energy-producing structures), rather than from the cell nucleus. Under metabolic stress conditions, it travels to the nucleus and activates AMPK — an enzyme that acts as a cellular energy sensor and regulator.

NAD+

Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide
99.5% HPLC · CAS 53-84-9

A coenzyme present in every living cell and essential for mitochondrial energy production and sirtuin enzyme activity. Sirtuins are proteins involved in DNA repair, gene regulation, and cellular stress responses.

Retatrutide

GLP-1 / GIP / Glucagon Triple Agonist
98.8% HPLC

A synthetic peptide that simultaneously activates three distinct metabolic receptors — GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon — each involved in regulating energy balance, insulin response, and fat metabolism. By engaging all three pathways at once, it offers researchers a tool for studying multi-receptor metabolic signalling in ways that single-agonist compounds cannot.
