Research overview

Research bundle combining three neuropeptides studied across distinct but related neurological pathways. Semax is investigated for BDNF expression and neuroprotection; Selank for anxiolytic-like profiles and serotonergic modulation; DSIP for its effects on slow-wave sleep architecture and stress-axis signalling. Together they cover cognitive, stress-response, and sleep regulation pathway research in a single bundle. Three individual lyophilised vials. For in vitro research use only.

Compounds in this stack

Semax

ACTH(4-7) Pro-Gly-Pro Analogue

A synthetic 7-amino-acid peptide based on a section of the stress hormone ACTH, with a stabilising tail added to improve its reach within biological systems. Preclinical studies have measured increased BDNF expression — a protein associated with brain cell growth and survival — in neuronal tissue following administration.

CAS 80714-61-0 99% HPLC View Semax →

Selank

Synthetic Heptapeptide (TKPRPGP)

A synthetic 7-amino-acid peptide based on a fragment of the immune protein tuftsin. Rodent studies have documented calmer behaviour in treated animals without observed sedation — a profile that distinguishes it from classical anxiolytic compounds in research settings.

CAS 129954-34-3 98.9% HPLC View Selank →

DSIP

Delta Sleep-Inducing Peptide

A 9-amino-acid neuropeptide first isolated from rabbit blood during deep sleep states. Animal studies investigate its effects on the architecture of sleep — particularly slow-wave and delta sleep stages — as well as its interaction with the body's stress-response axis.

CAS 62568-57-4 98.7% HPLC View DSIP →
