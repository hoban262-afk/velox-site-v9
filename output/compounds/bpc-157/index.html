Research overview

A 15-amino-acid synthetic peptide that is among the most extensively studied repair-focused compounds in preclinical science. Rodent studies have investigated its role in stimulating new blood vessel formation (angiogenesis), accelerating wound tissue closure, and protecting the stomach and intestinal lining from experimentally induced damage. It has also been studied for tendon and ligament tissue response and its interactions with growth factors such as VEGF. For in vitro research use only. HPLC purity 99.4%.

Summary paraphrased from the peer-reviewed preclinical literature. Full citations will be available in the research library. For in vitro research use only.
