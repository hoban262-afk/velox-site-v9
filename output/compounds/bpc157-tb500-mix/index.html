Research overview

A single lyophilised vial combining 10mg BPC-157 and 10mg TB-500 — the two most widely studied tissue-repair peptides in preclinical science. BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) is a 15-amino-acid synthetic peptide investigated in rodent models for roles in angiogenesis, wound tissue closure, and mucosal protection. TB-500 (synthetic thymosin beta-4 fragment) is studied for its actin-regulating properties and effects on cellular migration in tissue-response models. Combining both compounds in a single vial simplifies reconstitution and research protocols where both are used alongside each other. Supplied as a single 20mg co-lyophilised vial (10mg of each component). HPLC purity 98.5%.

Frequently Asked Research-Use Questions

What is in the BPC-157 & TB-500 Mix vial?
Each vial contains 10mg of BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) and 10mg of TB-500 (synthetic thymosin beta-4 fragment), co-lyophilised in a single sterile vial — 20mg total peptide content.
What purity is the BPC-157 & TB-500 Mix?
The BPC-157 & TB-500 Mix is supplied at 98.5% HPLC-verified purity for both components. A batch-specific certificate of analysis documenting purity, mass spectrometry confirmation, and appearance is provided with every order.
How is the BPC-157 & TB-500 Mix reconstituted for research use?
The mix is supplied as a co-lyophilised powder in a single sterile vial. For in vitro research use, reconstitution is performed with bacteriostatic water. See the reconstitution calculator for volume calculations based on your required concentration.
What is the difference between the BPC-157 & TB-500 Mix and the BPC-157 + TB-500 Bundle?
The Mix supplies both compounds co-lyophilised together in a single 20mg vial — one reconstitution step for both. The Bundle supplies BPC-157 and TB-500 as two separate individual vials (10mg each), allowing independent dosing and reconstitution of each compound. The Mix suits protocols where both compounds are always used together; the Bundle offers greater flexibility.
