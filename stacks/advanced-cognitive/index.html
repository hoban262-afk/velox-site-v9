Research overview

Research bundle combining three compounds studied across distinct but complementary neurological pathways. Semax is investigated for BDNF expression and neuroprotection in brain tissue models; Selank for anxiolytic-like profiles and serotonergic/GABAergic pathway modulation; Dihexa for HGF/c-Met receptor signalling and hippocampal synaptic density. Together they represent a broad-coverage neurological pathway research toolkit. Three individual lyophilised vials. For in vitro research use only.

Compounds in this stack

Semax

ACTH(4-7) Pro-Gly-Pro Analogue

A synthetic 7-amino-acid peptide based on a section of the stress hormone ACTH, with a stabilising tail added to improve its reach within biological systems. Preclinical studies have measured increased BDNF expression — a protein associated with brain cell growth and survival — in neuronal tissue following administration.

CAS 80714-61-0 99% HPLC View Semax →

Selank

Synthetic Heptapeptide (TKPRPGP)

A synthetic 7-amino-acid peptide based on a fragment of the immune protein tuftsin. Rodent studies have documented calmer behaviour in treated animals without observed sedation — a profile that distinguishes it from classical anxiolytic compounds in research settings.

CAS 129954-34-3 98.9% HPLC View Selank →

Dihexa

Angiotensin IV-Derived Oligopeptide

A synthetic peptide derived from an angiotensin IV analogue, engineered to resist enzymatic breakdown for greater stability in biological environments. Preclinical research investigates its interaction with the HGF/c-Met signalling axis — a pathway associated with the formation and strengthening of synaptic connections between brain cells.

CAS 1401708-83-5 98.5% HPLC View Dihexa →
