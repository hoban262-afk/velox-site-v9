Research overview

Research bundle combining three of the most studied tissue-focused peptides in preclinical repair research. BPC-157 is investigated for blood vessel formation, wound closure, and mucosal protection; TB-500 for actin regulation and cellular migration in tissue organisation; GHK-Cu for collagen synthesis and tissue remodelling. Together they cover vascular, cellular, and structural aspects of tissue repair pathway research. Three individual lyophilised vials. For in vitro research use only.

Compounds in this stack

BPC-157

Body Protection Compound-157

A 15-amino-acid synthetic peptide that is among the most extensively studied repair-focused compounds in preclinical science. Rodent studies have investigated its role in stimulating new blood vessel formation (angiogenesis), accelerating wound tissue closure, and protecting the stomach and intestinal lining from experimentally induced damage.

CAS 137525-51-0 99.4% HPLC View BPC-157 →

TB-500

Thymosin Beta-4

A synthetic version of Thymosin Beta-4, a protein naturally present in mammalian tissue. Preclinical studies investigate its role in regulating actin — a structural protein that controls how cells move and organise during tissue repair.

CAS 77591-33-4 98.8% HPLC View TB-500 →

GHK-Cu

Copper Tripeptide-1

A naturally occurring tripeptide found in mammalian blood that carries a copper atom. In skin cell and animal research models, it has been studied for its ability to upregulate the genes responsible for producing collagen and elastin — the structural proteins that give tissue its strength and elasticity.

CAS 49557-75-7 99% HPLC View GHK-Cu →
